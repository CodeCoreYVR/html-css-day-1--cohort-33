HTML elements are the building blocks of the language. As almost every element can be the parent of another element, it's more helpful to think of elements as parts of a tree structure.

A section is a thematic grouping of content, typically with a heading.

Almost any HTML element in the body of a page can be made to display in any way you wish using an appropriate stylesheet. So the choice of element used to add structure to your content is a semantic one, not a styling one!

There are three ways of inserting a style sheet:

  • External style sheet
  • Internal style sheet
  • Inline style

An inline style has the highest priority, and will override external and internal styles and browser defaults.

Abril Fatface and Josefin Sans

Typography design is best understood by analyzing other prominent websites to figure out why their text reads so well on the digital screen.

Web type needs clean, easy-to-read combinations that clearly define heading, subheading and body copy so readers can scan and identify what they want to read. It's always good to consider the essence you are trying to visually communicate with your audience. Choosing a typeface isn't just about whether you like it or not, it's about how the words in your copy look when the typeface is applied.

Using mixed weights of one typeface is an excellent way to enhance hierarchy and form some nuance. Many designers feel that sans-serif fonts are easier to read on the web. Serif fonts won't present a problem, so long as they're sized properly. Using a serif for your body copy can make more dense information easy to read. Paragraph font choice really depends on the type of website. Using an italic pronounces a word or subheading without having to change font-families. Round and narrow typefaces offset nicely against each other. Use light and bold versions of the same family for versatility.

Design hyperlinks so that they appear "clickable". Great link design is a lengthy subject. The link hover state should also be noticeable whenever a visitor hovers their mouse over a hyperlink.
